FAQ

Who is Konkura for?

Konkura isn't just for serious athletes - anyone can find or create challenges to suit their fitness and ability levels, and find similar people to enjoy friendly competition with. All you need is a desire to get fitter and be the best you can be, and the tiniest bit of competitive spirit.

If you're the sort of person who can't help speeding up a little when they see someone running or cycling up ahead, or who can't resist sneaking a look at what weight other gym users are lifting, then Konkura is for you!

We believe that the vast majority of people have a competitive spirit. However, for the purpose of market size estimation we assume that the top 10% most competitive people in the population of the US, UK, Canada, Australia, South Africa and New Zealand (i.e. primarily English-speaking nations with a strong competitive sporting heritage) are our primary target market (although we already have members in over 50 countries!). With combined population of 485m, this puts an upper bound on Konkura's potential audience of 48.5m. Achieving 2% market penetration would give Konkura close to 1m active members, with the opportunity to broaden the appeal and the geographic reach to grow significantly beyond this.

This doesn't look like an MVP! What's going on?

We've been working on Konkura for quite a while without the benefit of the Startup Engineering course, and have made plenty of mistakes. We have a fully functional, advanced social network with a huge degree of flexibility for users to create and take part in challenges, including private challenges amongst groups of friends, complex multi-event challenges and scoring involving entering your own score or other members voting for or rating your performance. It's gone a bit beyond an MVP!

We get great feedback from users, and have watched less-capable and (we believe) less compelling concepts such as Fitocracy and Tribesports go from strength to strength while we've struggled to bring Konkura to market without any resources. Help us make this a huge sport and fitness success!

How will Konkura make money?

Konkura is primarily advertising and sponsorship based, although there is potential for significant merchandising, add-on services and even premium membership income streams in the future. Direct advertising income will depend on building a large, active and attractive user base. Sporty, competitive people tend to have above average income and to spend on their sport and fitness interests, making the Konkura member base attractive to advertisers. For brands seeking closer engagement with customers, sponsored challenges or collections of such challenges ('channels') represent a great option for promoting sport and fitness.

What do we need investment for?

We've built the main website and a basic mobile version, and market it via Facebook and other social networks in the spare time from our day jobs. The site is now ripe for taking to the next level with a professional marketing campaign, full-time development staff and development of a mobile app which allows run/cycle tracking, rapid score updates from the gym, push notification of your friends' fitness activities and quick photo/video uploads.

  • Marketing budget: Marketing launch budget including in-house marketing leader for one year and budget for promotional activity, campus champions etc. - $250,000
